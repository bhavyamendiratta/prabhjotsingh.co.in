About

Known for his performances and work in the Delhi Theatre Circuit, Prabhjot is the Founder and Creative Cultivator of The Roots India. He has been practicing theatre since his school days and wants to promote theatre as a form of mainstream education. His venture The Roots India focuses on developing art forms and using performing arts to creatively impart knowledge, ensure artist survival & growth and focuses on creating political and socially relevant content. At the end The Roots India endeavours to spread happiness by helping people build on their inherent artistic tendencies, by helping spread better awareness of arts and in some small way build a humanistic society.

Prabhjot has completed his diploma in Human Resource Management & Guidance and Counselling. He graduated from S.G.T.B Khalsa College, Delhi University, where he was the President of “ANKUR” The Theatre Society of S.G.T.B Khalsa College, General Secretary of Commerce Association and Cultural Head of S.G.T.B College. He is also the founder of the University’s Performers Community (UPC) that aims to bring Delhi University performers and artists and alumni under one roof.

He has completed the acclaimed Theatre Appreciation Course from National School of Drama where he was part of a select set of students from across India. Eminent theatre personalities like Dr. Anuradha Kapoor, K. S. Rajendran, Dr. Tripurari Sharma, Dr. Waman Kendre lectured on different aspects of theatre. While playwrights like Satish Alekar and Asif Ali Khan introduced fine elements of playwriting through a workshop. Also, renowned theatre and television artists like Himani Shivpuri, M.K. Raina engaged students in different aspects of acting and multi-culturality.

He has extensive experience of performing in several theatre productions and workshops. He has been trained under some of the theatre stalwarts such as, Faizal Alkazi, Danish Hussain, Ramu Ramunathan, Ajit Singh Pahlawat, Suresh Shetty, Devender Sharma, Kuljeet Singh and VK Sharma.

During his college days, he was recipient of numerous awards for best actor, best street play performer and best director from different colleges of Delhi University. He was also felicitated for his contribution in the field of theatre by the Delhi University Student Union (DUSU). He also won an award for Best Design for his self scripted play “Bande”.

Prabhjot has acted in many full-length stage productions, such as Mantorang (collage of Manto's story), Mahabhoj (written by Manu Bhandari), Park (written by Manav Kaul), and an adaptation of Neil Simon’s “Rumors” (“Khusar Phusar”) and more. His play “Park” was widely appreciated and he was invited by the Prithvi Theatre, Mumbai for a performance. He has also acted in television commercials for companies like Flipkart, Kurkure, AIC electronics, Dream 11, and IPL.



Apart from acting Prabhjot has written and directed myriad stage and street plays including:

  • Seekh Kabab and Bande. His magnum opus Seekh Kabab, a tragicomedy on the 1984 Sikh genocide premiered at the LTG auditorium, New Delhi in October 2015 and has travelled across India.
  • Present sir (on prevailing issues in Education system).
  • Dharam (on questioning the stereotype of the religious system)
  • Handicap (about the “spineless” society)
  • Reservation Leela (critiquing the reservation system)
  • We the people (on active political participation)
  • Can't pay? Won't pay! (A farcical play about the prevailing economic system) was invited for a guest performance by Sahitya Kala Parishad in 2014 and has been performed in different venues within Delhi and outside.

Prabhjot has also worked with Wizcraft as the Asst. Director for the play on Baba Banda Singh Bahadur, “Degho Tegho Fateh” an event organised by the Central Government in association with the Punjab Government and managed by Wizcraft on the 300th martyrdom anniversary of Baba Banda Singh Bahadur. He was also the Associate Director of “Dastan-e- Punjab” which was performed at the Town Hall, Amritsar as part of the 50th Anniversary of Punjabi Suba.

Prabhjot is well-versed with the intricacies of stage work and has experience in light designing. He has done commendable work for plays such as Kujh Afsane, a Punjabi play; Prithvi, a play on the environment performed at the American Centre and more. He has successfully conceptualized and designed college theatre festivals.He was also the panellist of the Atelier Campus Theatre Festival, in which teams from all over India participate.

He was also the panellist of the Atelier Campus Theatre Festival, in which teams from all over India participate. He also conceptualized and curated Short Play Festival with intent of creating a space for theatre artists to experiment and explore the dimensions of a theatrical production. The festival was well received by everyone.

Over the past few years he has been invited as a faculty for theatre training and as a judge in theatre festivals of Delhi University and numerous other universities. He has conducted workshops in different universities like IIT Kanpur, BITS Pilani, Sharda University, IP University as well as colleges of Delhi University like S.G.T.B Khalsa College, Sri Venkateswara College, Bhagat Singh College, Deshbandhu College, Mata Sundri College and many more.

He has been associated with schools such as The Cambridge Foundation, Modern Era Convent, The Adhayyan Public School, Sanjiviya Model School and others as faculty for theatre in education. He has worked on skill development, body language, speech and many other aspects of the students. Apart from this Prabhjot has also conducted Teacher Training Program where in his session he worked with educators to come up with creative ways of teaching methodology & brainstorm ways to make experiential learning as part of the curriculum.

Prabhjot has worked with organisations like Teach For India, Yoganjali & Ammu Care and has endeavoured with them to reach to a new pinnacle so that the children can know what experiential learning is like.

He has conducted corporate trainings using theatre and other art forms with companies such as General Electric, Genpact, Blue Prints Publications, Ernst & Young, etc. His sessions focused on learning, development, skill building, employee introspection, creative enhancement, human resource development, etc.

Presently, he is working on two major stage productions; one of them is *Balcony by Jean Genet* and a solo performance about violence in 1984. He is also working on Murgh Mussalam a play on Islam phobia and creating different performing arts programs where people can come and develop their artistic skills.
